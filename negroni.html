You reach for a Negroni, but just before the drink is in your grasp, it is replaced by:

a glass,

a spoon,

1 ounce of gin,

1 ounce of sweet vermouth,

1 ounce of campari,

a twist of orange peel,

and ice cubes.

You grab the glass and fill it with the ice cubes, gin, vermouth, and campari. All of these elements, middling when tasted in singularity, become a potent apertif to stimulate one's appetite once stirred with a spoon for a half-minute.

You take a sip of this bittersweet mixture. Although good by itself, the aroma of this cocktail is dark and bold, with notes of vegetation from the campari and junipter berries from the gin. To brighten it up, you grab the orange peel, and run the rind against the rim of your glass, and top the mixture with it.
