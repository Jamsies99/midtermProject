As you approach the phojito, the drink shimmers, like a reflection on the sea, and then disappears. In it's place stands:

a muddler,

a glass,

a straw,

a cinnamon stick,

a star anise clove,

a thai chili pepper,

8 mint leaves,

2 ounces of setlzer,

2 ounces of white rum,

.5 ounce of lime juice,

a pot of lemongrass syrup,

and ice cubes.

Inside of the cup, you drop the mint leaves inside with .75 ounces of the lemongrass syrup and rum. The lemongrass syrup smells wildly exotic, as it has been simmered with a cup of sugar and water for 30 minutes, allowing for the citric flavors of the lemongrass to infuse with the syrup.

At this point, you face a daunting task that recquires great discipline: releasing the flavor and scents from the mint by pressing the mint using the muddler. Applying too much pressure during this extraction will result in chlorophyll being released, which will lead your expedition to a bitter end.

Despite this danger, you are fearless in the face of adversity. You chuckle with amusement when others would shudder in fear. The mint leaves are perfectly extracted. You then place a few ice cubes inside of the cube and then top of the drink with seltzer.

To further augment your drink, you go all the way and then a little more. By topping it with a chili pepper, star anise, and a cinnamon It would not be a far stretch to say that you embark on a flavor safari.
