As the saying goes: "Winner, Winner, Chicken Dinner", so in other words you, the winner, should always have on hand:

chicken legs

masa harina flour,

4 eggs,

paprika,

canola oil,

salt,

pepper

and cayenne pepper

You start by creating a dredging system. You lay out masa harina in one tray and beaten eggs in another. In the tray of masa harina, you season the flour with cayenne, paprika, and salt, because a true champion is able to keep cool while facing the heat.

You season the chicken with salt and pepper, because only a LOSER would let his chicken be underseasoned and you aren't a loser, right? You're a true boss, a true Champion.

Once the chicken is seasoned, you dip the legs into the masa tray, shaking off the excess flour before you plunge them into a deluge of eggs. You repeat this process again, to build the most crispy outside layer for your chicken, chockful of seasoning and flavor.

It's time: frying time. You heat up your oil until it reaches 350 degrees fahrenheit, and in the process, making your house smell like the baby of a waffle house dumpster mixed with the Gulf of Mexico in 2010. But you disregard the complaints from your suitemates about the wafting odors now permeating your surroundings. For you are a champion, and the simple minds of the general population could never fathom the genius vested in yourself. You place in your drumsticks and let out a sigh of content as the oil crackles and emitts the most satisfying bubbling noises.

You fry these legs until they are G.B.D, Golden, Brown, Delicious. After marveling over these golden nuggets of the culinary world, you place them on a plate lined with paper towel to sop up some of the grease. After all, a true winner is put together, not a sweaty slob.

You eat almost immediately after the chicken has been drained of oil, for humble people serve the food, hungry people are the ones who eat.
